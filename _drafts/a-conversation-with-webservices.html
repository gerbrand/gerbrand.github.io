I once had to integrate a point of sale (POS) device with a payment device, with which I could only communicate via https. The POS was a tablet, while the payment device was an external device that allowed credit-and debit-card payment.
Communication between the POS en payment device required several webservice-calls, of which the next call depended on the result of the previous call.

For example after starting the transaction, the user could hit cancel on the payment device, sometimes he needed to enter a pincode and sometimes the payment device would report that the user would write down a signature. The person that used the POS, the sales-agent, would also have the ability to cancel the transaction which of course would have to be shown on the payment device.
The payment device didn't support callbacks, therefore I needed to poll the payment device frequently for status-updates.
Of course I did not want the tablet to block while handling communication with the payment device. On the other hand I wanted that the person who'd used the POS (the sales-person) would see status updates of the payment device immediately.
